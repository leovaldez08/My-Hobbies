My Hobbies

~ Rishi Ganesh

In today’s fast and competitive world, we often get time for ourselves. Over time, our schedule gets very dull and monotonous. That is why we need to indulge in something in between to keep our minds fresh and active. What’s better than a hobby for this? One of the main benefits of having a hobby is that it is a major stress-buster. You actually enjoy doing it and it satisfies your soul.

In other words, without a hobby, your life becomes an unhealthy cycle lacking any excitement or spark. Hobbies offer you a great opportunity to take a break and forget the worries of your life. They allow you to explore yourself and realize your potential in different areas.

Here are few hobbies of mine which I love to do when I'm bored or when I can spare some free time:

Boxing

Every time I wear my boxing gloves, I can feel the flow of adrenaline rushing through my body. Boxing is something challenging, as you have to be able to strike your opponents and at the same time to defend yourself. It’s great exercise and there’s nothing more refreshing than a warm shower after a hard workout. Once you learn the basic techniques and to defend yourself in a proper way, you’re ready to jack yourself in the ring, if you want to achieve greatness in boxing. I was about 11 years old when my father taught me the most basic move, “the jab”. Quick punch with either hand can deal some good damage to an unaware foe. If you manage to hit to the right place, you can end the match with one simple punch! My father also taught me the basic combination, “the L-L-R” or simply left-left-right.

Like in any hobby, boxing has its good sides and bad sides. Some of the good sides are; boxing is great exercise. You’ll have to move your whole body to get the full use of different moves, which means that you also train your other muscles. You also have to be swift in order to dodge, so practicing dodging also trains your speed and reflexes.But unfortunately, sometimes boxing can be really harsh and violent and can be even fatal too. But for me, boxing is just a hobby. A hobby that I love. I won’t use it to whoop any poor guy, but rather to defend myself. (P.S: I also know other martial arts like Nunchaku and Silambam)

Numismatics

Over the years coin collecting has been called the “Hobby of Kings” because only kings could afford to collect rare coins. It has now morphed into “The King of Hobbies” because everyone can now afford to collect coins. Coin collecting is a hobby that can last a lifetime because there are always new coins to collect. It started for me some years back when I used to take my Dad's coin collection book and look at coins of different countries. I started collecting like him in my money box and after that I always used to wait for my Dad to get back from work so that I can show my collection. Since then, it has become one of my favourite hobby.

This hobby has given me information about different countries of the world. I have got to know about important personalities of the different countries. It's really exciting to have them and keeping them safe. This hobby has made me responsible to take care of important things at very young age. I have also learnt dealings of different currencies on my foreign tours and save money for my favourite hobby. I feel extreme pleasure in showing my collection to others.
